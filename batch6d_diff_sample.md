## Graham's 10 Year Tawny

**Peak** (excellent):

- **Creme Brulee** — 10-year oak-cask oxidative Tawny — Graham's entry-point, dried apricot, walnut, toffee, gentle spice, smooth finish. The toffee echoes the torched-sugar crust directly and the walnut plays with vanilla-custard depth. Peak Tawny for creme brulee — caramelized-on-caramelized textbook match.
- **Chocolate Brownie** — 10-year oak-cask oxidative Tawny — Graham's entry-point, dried apricot, walnut, toffee, gentle spice, smooth finish. The toffee wraps around fudge-brownie cocoa depth and the walnut plays with whipped-chocolate frosting. Peak Tawny for chocolate brownie — Tawny smooths the cocoa intensity.

**Strong** (strong):

- **Mocha Creme** — 10-year oak-cask oxidative Tawny — Graham's entry-point, dried apricot, walnut, toffee, gentle spice. The toffee plays with espresso-yolk custard depth and the walnut echoes coffee-roast bitterness. Strong Tawny for mocha creme — Vintage Port's chocolate intensity edges higher on the espresso pour.
- **Chocolate Cake** — 10-year oak-cask oxidative Tawny — Graham's entry-point, dried apricot, walnut, toffee, gentle spice. The toffee plays with fudgy chocolate-cake crumb and the walnut bridges buttermilk-dairy depth. Strong Tawny for chocolate cake — Vintage Port's structure handles cocoa-intensity harder.

**Works** (works):

- **Frangelico** — 10-year oak-cask oxidative Tawny — Graham's entry-point, dried apricot, walnut, toffee, gentle spice. The walnut echoes Frangelico's hazelnut-liqueur directly, but pouring a nutty liqueur next to a nutty tawny doubles down and flattens both. Works post-dessert — pour one at a time for distinct experiences.

**Never pair** (avoid):

- **The Tomahawk** — Never pair Graham's 10 Year Tawny with Tomahawk — sweet oxidative port buries the massive 40oz bone-in ribeye and the residual sugar clashes with smoky char. Lead with Scavino Barolo or Beaucastel Châteauneuf for the grand cut. Tawny is a finishing pour, not a steak wine.
- **Scavino Barolo** — Never pour Graham's 10 Year Tawny alongside Scavino Barolo — sweet oxidative tawny next to bone-dry firm-tannin Barolo confuses the palate and the residual sugar flattens Barolo's tar-and-rose lift. Pour the Barolo with steak, the Tawny with dessert. Sequence matters.

**Strong** (strong):

- **Mocha Creme** — 20-year oak-cask oxidative Tawny — Graham's step-up, concentrated dried fruit, roasted hazelnut, orange peel, silky long finish. The roasted hazelnut echoes espresso-roast directly and the orange peel lifts yolk-cream custard. Strong step-up Tawny for mocha creme — Vintage Port's intensity edges higher on espresso.

**Works** (works):

- **Frangelico** — 20-year oak-cask oxidative Tawny — Graham's step-up, concentrated dried fruit, roasted hazelnut, orange peel, silky long finish. The roasted hazelnut echoes Frangelico's hazelnut-liqueur directly, doubling down flattens both. Works post-dessert — pour one at a time so the twenty-year Tawny gets its own moment.

**Never pair** (avoid):
