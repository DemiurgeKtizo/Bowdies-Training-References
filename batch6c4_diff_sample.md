## Lunaria Coste di Moro

**Peak** (excellent):

- **Kansas City** — Abruzzo Montepulciano d'Abruzzo — Lunaria earthy grippy, dark cherry, dried herbs, rustic medium body, medium tannin. - **Truffle Fries** — Abruzzo Montepulciano d'Abruzzo — Lunaria earthy grippy, dark cherry, dried herbs, rustic medium body, medium tannin. The medium tannin cuts truffle-fry potato fat and the dried herbs play against the truffle-oil umami. Peak Italian-approachable red for truffle fries — rustic Italian character on the umami hit.

**Strong** (strong):

- **Creamed Spinach** — Abruzzo Montepulciano d'Abruzzo — Lunaria earthy grippy, dark cherry, dried herbs, rustic medium body, medium tannin. The medium tannin cuts béchamel's dairy weight and the dried herbs play against the spinach base. Strong Italian-approachable red for creamed spinach — rustic character reframes the rich side.
- **Cowboy Ribeye** — Abruzzo Montepulciano d'Abruzzo — Lunaria earthy grippy, dark cherry, dried herbs, rustic medium body, medium tannin. The medium body handles ribeye's big marbled build though rustic medium tannin reads softer than firm-tannin Cabs on the cut. Strong Italian-approachable red for ribeye — rustic value on the bold cut.

**Works** (works):

- **House Wedge** — Abruzzo Montepulciano d'Abruzzo — Lunaria earthy grippy, dark cherry, dried herbs, rustic medium body, medium tannin. The medium tannin cuts iceberg-and-bacon and the dark cherry plays against blue cheese dressing. Works as Italian-approachable red for the wedge — rustic value reframes the classic salad.
- **Lardons** — Abruzzo Montepulciano d'Abruzzo — Lunaria earthy grippy, dark cherry, dried herbs, rustic medium body, medium tannin. The medium tannin cuts lardons' bacon-fat richness and the dried herbs play against the smoky pork. Works as Italian-approachable red for lardons — rustic character on the rich topper.

**Never pair** (avoid):

- **Crab Cake** — Rustic Abruzzo Montepulciano against crab cake's delicate sweet meat — Lunaria's earthy medium-tannin grip buries the shellfish. Never pair. Route crab cake to Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Rosé, or Champagne; keep Lunaria for filet, strip, or rustic meat courses.
- **Laurent Perrier Le Cuvée Brut** — Rustic Abruzzo Montepulciano alongside Laurent Perrier Champagne — earthy grippy weight against bubbly acid is incoherent as a flight. Never layer. Keep Lunaria for the meat course; route Laurent Perrier to aperitif, seafood, or light-starter service where Champagne peaks.

## Kermit Lynch Côtes du Rhône

**Peak** (excellent):

- **Kansas City** — Rhône Valley Côtes du Rhône Grenache-Syrah-Mourvèdre — Kermit Lynch, red cherry, wild herbs, pepper, garrigue, medium body. The pepper note echoes Kansas City's lean-bold beef char and the wild herbs lift against grilled strip. Peak Southern-French for the strip — a mid-weight garrigue match.
- **Grilled Caesar** — Rhône Valley Côtes du Rhône Grenache-Syrah-Mourvèdre — Kermit Lynch, red cherry, wild herbs, pepper, garrigue, medium body. The wild herbs match anchovy-lemon dressing and the pepper cuts through parm. Peak Southern-French for grilled caesar — Kermit Lynch food-wine handles the salad beautifully.

**Strong** (strong):

- **Filet Mignon** — Rhône Valley Côtes du Rhône Grenache-Syrah-Mourvèdre — Kermit Lynch, red cherry, wild herbs, pepper, garrigue, medium body. The medium body matches butter-tender filet's delicate weight and the wild herbs play against escargot-butter. Strong Southern-French for filet — a lighter-touch Rhône pairing.
- **Sauteed Garlic Spinach** — Rhône Valley Côtes du Rhône Grenache-Syrah-Mourvèdre — Kermit Lynch, red cherry, wild herbs, pepper, garrigue, medium body. The wild herbs echo garlic-oil aromatics and the medium body matches spinach-iron. Strong Southern-French for garlic spinach — Rhône herb-on-herb harmony.

**Works** (works):

- **Cowboy Ribeye** — Rhône Valley Côtes du Rhône Grenache-Syrah-Mourvèdre — Kermit Lynch, red cherry, wild herbs, pepper, garrigue, medium body. The medium body is slightly light for Cowboy's marbled 22oz bone-in ribeye, but the pepper matches grilled char. Works for ribeye — Grand Tinel's Châteauneuf full-body handles the cut harder.
- **Au Gratin Potatoes** — Rhône Valley Côtes du Rhône Grenache-Syrah-Mourvèdre — Kermit Lynch, red cherry, wild herbs, pepper, garrigue, medium body. The pepper cuts gruyere-cream richness and the medium body handles dairy weight. Works for au gratin — Muga Reserva's vanilla-leather plays with the cheese harder.

**Never pair** (avoid):

- **Seared Scallops** — Never pair Kermit Lynch Rhône with seared scallops — pepper and garrigue overwhelm delicate sweet shellfish and the medium body clashes with browned-butter. Lead with Clos de la Chapelle Chardonnay or Rombauer's creamy richness for sear-seared harmony.
- **Crab Cake** — Never pair Kermit Lynch Rhône with crab cake — wild herbs and pepper overwhelm delicate lump crab and the garrigue bury the remoulade. Lead with Heitz Sauvignon Blanc's citrus lift or Reverdy Sancerre's mineral edge for shellfish clarity.
