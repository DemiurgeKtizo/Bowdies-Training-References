## Headline

The engine is structurally clean — 9/9 health checks pass, mirror integrity perfect, no cooking-method drift, FxF snapshot unchanged. **But the underlying corpus has accumulated heavy phrase recycling that the current `isTemplatedNote()` regex set fails to catch, plus residual factual drift on two steak weights and a population of 3,888 legacy-format notes that lack verdict labels entirely.** The "47.1% templated" health-check headline understates the problem: it counts loose connectives like `sits alongside`. The regen-script signature set catches only 6.9% of what is plausibly engine-templated. The gap between the two — roughly 22,000 notes — is the hidden recycled tier.

If I had to pick three things to ship next:
1. Lock down the recycled bourbon-class body skeleton (5,610 occurrences of one phrase).
2. Sweep the 8oz/16oz weight drift into `fix_steak_weights_in_editorial.js`.
3. Regenerate the 3,888 verdict-label-less legacy notes.

### What this means

A single 6-word body phrase — `the bourbon depth settles on the plate` — appears in 10% of the entire corpus. That is an order of magnitude past acceptable. It originates in the `BOURBON_BOLD` class body template and got baked into editorial during a prior regen. Because the current `isTemplatedNote()` regex set in `regenerate_dxf_notes.js` doesn't include this phrase as a signature, those 5,000+ notes are now classified as "editorial" and won't be rewritten on regen — they're frozen in.

The same pattern holds for the bridge-verb pool. **Eleven verbs each appear 1,000–5,000 times**, and they cycle through a small set of bottle character → food character templates:

```
[Bottle]'s [character] {sits alongside | leans against | reads alongside |
 stays alongside | sits beside | plays cleanly against | holds with |
 finds neutral with | overpowers | frames | underclubs} the [food]'s [edge] —
[the bourbon depth settles on the plate], and the [tail clause].
```

12% of the corpus (6,076 notes) opens with the `X's [adj] character` pattern. The opening is fine; it's that the body skeleton it leads into is fixed.

### Recommendation

The 2026-04-27 v6.2 audit cited the `flavorPattern()` variant pool fix as the FxF answer to recycling. The same architectural fix needs to land for DxF body construction:

1. Add the recycled body phrases to the `isTemplatedNote()` signature regex in `regenerate_dxf_notes.js`. Specifically: `bourbon depth settles on the plate`, `vanilla layers into the cream`, `pairing sits at neutral register`, `call lands as a measured alongside`, `call holds neither soars nor fights`, `pairing reads as a quiet alongside`. This unfreezes ~10K notes for regen.
2. Expand `BOURBON_BOLD` (and other class) body templates from one anchor phrase to a deterministic-hash variant pool of 6–10, matching the FxF `PATTERN_VARIANTS` model.
3. Re-run regen → mirror sync → snapshot test → lock.

Estimated impact: the top-10 recycled phrases drop from 30,000+ aggregate hits to ~3,000–5,000.

## 3 · Templated detection inconsistency

The two "is this templated?" detectors in the codebase disagree heavily:

| Detector | Templated count | % |
|---|---:|---:|
| `engine_health_check.js` SIGS (15 patterns) | 24,130 | 47.1% |
| `regenerate_dxf_notes.js` SIGS (33 patterns) | 3,530 | 6.9% |
| Both match | 1,734 | 3.4% |
| Neither match (truly editorial) | 25,316 | 49.4% |

Health-check signatures include broad connectives (`/sits alongside/`, `/leans against/`) that legitimately appear in human-written editorial. Regen-script signatures are tight and only catch the most obvious template tells (`runs straight into`, `Save the X for the steak course`). The result:

- Health check **over-counts** templated, masking real corpus health.
- Regen scripts **under-count** templated, leaving recycled body phrases frozen in place.
- ~22,000 notes sit in the gap — they look editorial to the regen pipeline but are clearly engine-shaped on inspection.

### Recommendation

Pick one canonical `isTemplatedNote()` and import it everywhere from `pairing_engine_taxonomy.js` (or a new `engine/templated_detection.js` module). Mirror the FxF discipline already established for `drinkClassFor` / `foodClassFor`. Update both the health-check and the regen scripts to use it.

## 4 · Verdict label consistency

### 3,888 notes (7.6%) have no recognizable verdict label

These are legacy-format notes (pre-current-engine) shaped like:

> *Filet Mignon: tenderloin meets emmer-wheat wheated bourbon. Old Emmer's ancient-grain wheated character frames tenderloin with distinctive craft-bourbon register — parallel to Weller 12 and Basil Hayden's approaches…*

No `Strong;` / `Excellent;` / `Works;` closer, no `Gold standard;` / `Peak X for Y` opener. The text is often quite good editorial, but the absence of a tier marker means the front-end has no quick verdict bubble to render and the audit script can't classify them.

Distribution by map tier:
```
2,996  excellent
  426  strong
  172  avoid
  138  gold       ← these are the most painful ones
  104  works
  ─────
3,836  total
```

### Tier-text disagreements (refined detection)

After excluding false positives from "Atlas Peak", "Peak Cab", "peak higher (verb)", and the intentional gold-subset-of-excellent overlap, real disagreements remain:

| Map tier | Note tier | Count |
|---|---|---:|
| gold | excellent | 74 |
| gold | strong | 18 |
| gold | works | 10 |
| excellent | strong | 52 |
| excellent | works | 18 |
| excellent | gold | 164 (likely mostly resolved-as-gold pairs in both lists) |
| strong | works | 52 |
| strong | gold | (small) |
| works | strong | 4 |
| Total real disagreements (excluding null-text) | **~250** |

### Gold-tier under-promotion is the standout

20% of gold-tier pairs (~240/1,164) carry an "Excellent" or no-label verdict — they're the engine's most important calls (the recommendation a server actually leads with), and the text undersells the tier. Examples:

- `Cowboy Ribeye | Heaven Hill 20 Year` (gold) — note ends `Strong; HH 20's aged depth frames the ribeye at collector register.`
- `Truffle Fries | Pommery Cuvée Louise` (gold) — `Excellent; prestige champagne's truffle-affinity is textbook…`
- `Seafood Tower | Bombay Dry Gin` (gold) — `Excellent; Bombay Dry's traditional character delivers the raw bar classic-gin experience.`

### Recommendation

Build a one-pass corrector: walk every gold-listed pair, verify the note opens with `Gold standard;` / `Peak X for Y` or contains it in the verdict; for offenders, regenerate the verdict using the gold-tier corpus pool. Same for excellent → null/strong drifts. The audit script `engine/audit_tier_note_mismatches.js` already exists for COURSE_TO_DESSERT (and finds 0); generalize it to all tier intersections.

## 5 · Factual drift — steak weights

The cooking-method check is clean (0 drift). But quantitative weight callouts have drifted:

| Steak | Canonical (CLAUDE.md) | Drift in notes | Notes affected |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Filet Mignon | **10oz** | "8oz" cut/lean | **142** |
| Kansas City | **18oz** | "16oz" strip | **164** |
| Cowboy Ribeye | 26oz | (none) | 0 ✅ |
| Tomahawk | 36oz | (none in current notes) | 0 ✅ |
| Porterhouse | 40oz | (none) | 0 ✅ |
| Bone-In Filet | 14oz | (none) | 0 ✅ |

**Total: 306 notes carry the wrong steak weight.**

`engine/fix_steak_weights_in_editorial.js` handles `22oz → 26oz`, `40oz → 36oz` (Tomahawk-context only), and `6oz → 10oz` Filet patterns — but doesn't include `8oz → 10oz` Filet or `16oz → 18oz` Kansas City. The new drift is from a different editorial vintage that wasn't covered when the script was last extended.

### Recommendation

Extend `fix_steak_weights_in_editorial.js` with these patterns (carefully — `16` and `8` are common numbers):

```js
// Filet 8oz → 10oz (only in Filet-context phrases)
['8oz cut',                '10oz cut'],         // appears in "against the 8oz cut"
['8oz lean',               '10oz lean'],
['8oz tenderloin',         '10oz tenderloin'],
['lean butter-tender 8oz', 'lean butter-tender 10oz'],
['the 8oz filet',          'the 10oz filet'],
['8oz center-cut',         '10oz center-cut'],
// Kansas City 16oz → 18oz
['16oz strip',             '18oz strip'],       // appears in "against a 16oz strip"
['16-oz strip',            '18-oz strip'],
['the 16oz KC',            'the 18oz KC'],
['the 16oz Kansas',        'the 18oz Kansas'],
```

After extending, run the script, then re-snapshot.

## 6 · Slot-fill bug in closer templates

`Beignets | Pablo Sour` (a dessert + cocktail pairing) closes with:

> *…the bright build frames the beignets, and the citrus cuts the dessert. **Strong; the side carries its register against the cut.***

"the side carries its register against the cut" is a SIDE × STEAK closer — wrong dish-class slot fill. Beignets is a dessert; Pablo Sour is a cocktail. The closer template appears to default to STEAK_SIDE phrasing when the actual archetype is DESSERT_DRINK.

This is a one-off observation from the snapshot drift. A scan for `the side carries its register` and similar SIDE-anchored closers in non-side contexts would surface the population.

## 7 · Older / "legacy-format" notes

5,128 notes (10%) follow the older shape:

> *1792 Small Batch with the KC — Barton 1792 small-batch against the strip's marbling. The spicy-sweet high-rye handles the cut at the balanced mid-proof.*

vs. current shape:

> *1792 Small Batch's caramel-spice character sits beside the strip's marbling — the bourbon depth settles on the plate, and the rounded sweetness softens the side. Strong; …*

The legacy notes are often higher-quality writing (more specific, less recycled) but lack:
- A verdict label
- The current bridge-verb / body-phrase template — which is the *cause* of the recycling problem in §2.

There's a real tension here: the legacy notes are stylistically better, but mechanically inconsistent. The right move is probably:

1. Promote the best legacy notes back into hand-curated editorial (mark them as preserved, append a verdict label).
2. Regenerate the rest using the (post-fix) v7 generator.

Don't blanket-overwrite. The legacy notes are a corpus quality asset.

The corpus has a real recycling problem at the body-phrase layer, and a residual factual-drift / verdict-label tail. Neither is structural; both are addressable with targeted regen passes against an updated `isTemplatedNote()`.
