## Scotto Cellars The Lost Chapters

**Peak** (excellent):

- **Kansas City** — California Cab by-the-glass value — black cherry, cassis, dark chocolate, integrated medium tannin, smooth full finish. The smooth structure meets Kansas City's lean-bold marbled strip and the dark chocolate plays against beefy char. Peak by-the-glass Cab for the strip — accessible California value on the American cut.
- **House Wedge** — California Cab by-the-glass — black cherry, cassis, dark chocolate, integrated medium tannin, smooth. The medium tannin cuts iceberg-and-bacon and the dark chocolate plays against blue cheese dressing. Peak by-the-glass Cab for the wedge — accessible California value reframes the classic salad.

**Strong** (strong):

- **Truffle Fries** — California Cab by-the-glass — black cherry, cassis, dark chocolate, integrated medium tannin. The medium tannin cuts truffle-fry potato fat and the dark chocolate plays against the truffle-oil umami. Strong by-the-glass Cab for truffle fries — accessible California value on the umami hit.
- **Brussels and Belly** — California Cab by-the-glass — black cherry, cassis, dark chocolate, integrated medium tannin. The medium tannin cuts pork-belly fat and the dark chocolate plays against charred brussels. Strong by-the-glass Cab for Brussels and Belly — accessible California value on the rich starter.

**Works** (works):

- **Cowboy Ribeye** — California Cab by-the-glass — black cherry, cassis, dark chocolate, integrated medium tannin, full body. The full body meets ribeye's big marbled build though medium tannin reads softer than firm-tannin Napa Cabs on the cut. Works as by-the-glass Cab for ribeye — accessible value when guest wants Cab at the price point.
- **Lardons** — California Cab by-the-glass — black cherry, cassis, dark chocolate, integrated medium tannin. The medium tannin cuts lardons' bacon-fat richness and the dark chocolate plays against the smoky pork. Works as by-the-glass Cab for lardons — accessible California on the rich topper.

**Never pair** (avoid):

- **Crab Cake** — By-the-glass California Cab against crab cake's delicate sweet meat — full-body medium-tannin structure buries the shellfish. Never pair. Route crab cake to Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Rosé, or Champagne; keep Lost Chapters for filet, strip, or the steak course.
- **Burrata** — By-the-glass California Cab against burrata's creamy-delicate center — full-body cassis-chocolate structure overwhelms the starter. Never pair. Route burrata to Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Rosé, or Prosecco; keep Lost Chapters for the steak course.
